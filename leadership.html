AGSSA–ISSMGE (South Asia Chapter)

General Secretary (2025 – Present)

As General Secretary, I represent regional geotechnical interests, foster collaboration among South Asian member societies, and strengthen global engagement. I played a major role in establishing this South Asia Chapter of AGSSA–ISSMGE, creating a platform that enhances the visibility of both the society and Nepal in international forums. This chapter promotes knowledge exchange, professional development, and regional cooperation within the field of geotechnical engineering.

Nepal Engineers' Association (NEA)

Past Executive Member (2014 - 2016)

Elected with the highest votes, contributed to national disaster initiatives and policy development. Key leadership highlights:

  • Gorkha Earthquake (2015) – NEA Rapid Damage Assessment: helped design, coordinate, and deliver the nationwide program that trained 1,000+ engineers for rapid damage assessment and deployment.
  • Dang Engineers Association engagement: facilitated collaboration with members of parliament to contribute to the Dang Development Road Map.
  • Provincial leadership capacity-building: worked with Pradesh Presidents to deliver training on democratic governance, organizational strengthening, and the role of engineers in Nepal’s development.

Center of Democratic Engineers Nepal (CDEN)

Past Vice-President (2078 B.S - 2080 B.S)

Nepal Geotechnical Society (NGS)

Leadership Roles (2018–2025)

General Secretary (2018–2020)

As General Secretary, contributed to national geotechnical research and disaster risk reduction initiatives. Led collaborative projects with diverse stakeholders, promoted knowledge sharing, and supported DRR programs. Focused on strengthening resilience and advancing geotechnical practices for sustainable development in Nepal while building partnerships for impactful outcomes.

Vice President (2020–2023)

As Vice President, advanced national geotechnical research and supported disaster risk reduction strategies. Encouraged innovation, collaboration, and professional development within the society. Worked to strengthen community resilience, foster partnerships, and guide impactful initiatives contributing to sustainable infrastructure development and effective disaster management practices in Nepal.

President (2023–2025)

Led the organization with a two-year strategic plan, making NGS a vibrant and active professional society. Focused on national and international collaboration, public awareness, and knowledge sharing in geotechnical engineering and geohazards. Played a key role in establishing the Association of Geotechnical Societies in South Asia (AGSSA) under the ISSMGE framework.

GeoMandu 2023

Conference Convenor (2023)

Convened the biannual conference “Geohazards and Geodisasters”, uniting 45 international and 200+ national participants. Oversaw 46 technical paper presentations, keynote sessions, and collaborative discussions. Established GeoMandu 2023 as a premier South Asian platform for geotechnical knowledge exchange, professional networking, and advancing disaster risk reduction practices.

GeoMandu 2024

Conference Convenor (2024)

Convened “Geotechnics for Sustainable Infrastructures”, drawing 500+ participants with 170+ international delegates. Directed over 150 presentations, including keynotes, distinguished lectures, and technical sessions. Strengthened global collaboration, showcased innovative geotechnical research, and positioned GeoMandu 2024 as a leading platform for sustainable infrastructure dialogue and international knowledge exchange.
