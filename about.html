A Lifelong Journey in Engineering and Service

Growing up in the rugged hills of Rolpa, where resources were scarce but dreams were abundant, I learned early on the value of perseverance and purpose. My early education was shaped by determination as much as by textbooks, and the desire to build something meaningful carried me through my studies in civil engineering across various Tribhuvan University institutions. Years later, that journey culminated in a PhD in Geotechnical Engineering - a milestone not just for me, but for the communities I aimed to serve.

My professional path has been defined by both fieldwork and scholarship. I have contributed to post-earthquake damage assessments, investigated liquefaction risks, and worked on bridge projects designed for resilience in Nepal’s challenging terrain. Alongside this technical work, I have taught and mentored hundreds of aspiring engineers, helping them navigate not just the complexities of engineering science, but also the responsibilities that come with serving society.

“The problems engineers face in Nepal are not technical alone - they are institutional, social, and systemic. That’s where I focus my energy.”

Beyond projects and classrooms, I have taken on institutional roles not for the recognition, but to create platforms where others can grow. My belief is that the challenges facing engineers in Nepal are rarely purely technical - they are deeply rooted in institutional, social, and systemic realities.
